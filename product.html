Jasmine is a fragrant flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae (olive) family. Known for its sweet and pleasant aroma, jasmine flowers are typically small, white or yellow, and bloom abundantly in warm climates. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, especially in Asia, jasmine is widely cultivated for ornamental use, essential oils, and perfumes. In many cultures, it symbolizes purity, love, and beauty, and is used in religious ceremonies, teas, and traditional medicine.
